Ada Lovelace was an English mathematician best known for her work on Charles Babbage's proposed Analytical Engine. Her notes included what is widely regarded as the first published computer algorithm.

Lovelace recognized that computing machines could potentially manipulate symbols and information beyond numerical calculations, making her an important visionary in the history of computing.
